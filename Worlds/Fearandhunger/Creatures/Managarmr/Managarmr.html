Description

Managarmr is one of the Old Gods of *Fear & Hunger*, a primordial deity representing the ceaseless cycle of fear, hunger, and decay. Its form is a grotesque amalgamation of bestial and humanoid traits—a skeletal, wolf-like entity with elongated limbs and hollow eyes that reflect the void. It is said to gnaw at the roots of the world tree, ensuring entropy and suffering endure. Unlike other gods, Managarmr does not seek worship; it thrives on the base instincts of mortals. Its presence is felt in every starving wretch, every coward’s retreat, and every act of desperation. The deeper one delves into the dungeons of Fear & Hunger, the stronger its influence grows, warping minds and flesh alike.

"To face Managarmr is to stare into the abyss of your own survival instinct—and find it staring back, hungry." — Lost Scholar of the Ma’habre Library

Clasification

  • Class: Old God (Primordial)
  • Threat Level: Existential
  • Contact Risk: Soul Corruption / Flesh Warping
  • Presence Detected: Dungeon Depths, Starvation Zones, Sacrificial Pits

Scholar’s Report – Ma’habre Fragment #666-XXXII

"Managarmr is not a being to be bargained with—it is the embodiment of the dungeon’s law: *Eat or be eaten*. Its ‘blessings’ manifest as mutations: elongated jaws, hollow stomachs, and an insatiable craving for flesh. Survivors report hearing its howls in the deepest tunnels, though no natural beast could produce such a sound. It is said that those who starve to death in the dungeon become part of its ever-growing mass, their souls trapped in an eternal cycle of hunger. Warning: Direct eye contact induces irreversible paranoia. 87% of test subjects began self-cannibalization within 3 hours."

Survivor Advisory

  • Do NOT attempt rituals without Sulfur protection.
  • If you dream of teeth grinding against bone, sever all ties with your party.
  • First sign of corruption: Teeth growing unnaturally sharp.
  • Second sign: Hearing whispers in a language that doesn’t exist.
  • Final sign: Your shadow no longer matches your movements.

Supplemental Notes

Ancient texts suggest Managarmr was once bound by the New Gods, but its essence leaked into the dungeon’s foundation. The ‘Hunger’ mechanic in the game is a direct manifestation of its influence—the longer you resist eating, the stronger its pull becomes. Curiously, its name shares roots with Norse mythology’s *Managarmr*, the wolf destined to devour the moon during Ragnarök. Whether this is coincidence or a fragment of lost knowledge is unknown. Recent cult activity indicates attempts to ‘feed’ Managarmr with entire villages—a ritual dubbed ‘The Great Gnawing.’ Survivors are never found, only echoes of their screams.
